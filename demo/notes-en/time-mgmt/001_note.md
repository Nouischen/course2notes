# 1.1 Why you always feel short on time
> Course: Time Management in Practice (sample)

## Key points
- Running out of time usually isn't about *hours* — it's about **scattered attention** and **no priorities**.
- Most people overestimate what they can do in a day and underestimate what they can do in a week — so they're always behind.
- The first move isn't doing more; it's **deciding what NOT to do**.

## Notes

**Three real time thieves**
- **Switching cost**: every interruption costs ~10+ minutes to get back into flow. It looks like "just a second," but the loss is huge.
- **Fake productivity**: busy all day, replying to lots of messages — while moving nothing that actually matters.
- **No boundaries**: if anyone can reach you anytime, your day fills up with other people's urgencies.

**One mindset shift**
> You don't "have no time." You just haven't decided whether this thing is important enough.

## Takeaways
- Make a **not-to-do list** today: three things you'll stop or hand off.
- Turn off notifications; keep only the truly urgent sources.
