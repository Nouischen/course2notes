# 1.3 The Pomodoro technique & deep work
> Course: Time Management in Practice (sample)

## Key points
- **Deep work** — long, uninterrupted, focused stretches — is what produces high-value output, and it needs to be protected on purpose.
- The **Pomodoro** is the easiest way in: 25 minutes focused, 5 minutes rest. Use rhythm to beat distraction.
- Two to four real deep-work blocks a day already beats most people.

## Notes

**How to run a Pomodoro**
1. Pick one task, set 25 minutes, and do only that.
2. If another thought pops up, write it on paper and come back later — don't jump out now.
3. When the timer ends, take 5 minutes and leave your seat.
4. After 4 Pomodoros, take a longer break (15–30 minutes).

**Three conditions that make deep work happen**
- **Fixed time slot**: same time each day so your brain forms the habit.
- **Remove temptation**: phone away, notifications off, only the tabs you need.
- **A clear output**: before you start, write down what "done" looks like for this block.

> Focus is a muscle: at first you can only hold 25 minutes; with practice you can dive for 90.

## Takeaways
- Schedule one 25-minute Pomodoro tomorrow morning for your most important task.
- Keep a "distraction parking" sheet for thoughts that show up mid-session.
