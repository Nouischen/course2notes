# 1.2 The Eisenhower Matrix: separate "urgent" from "important"
> Course: Time Management in Practice (sample)

## Key points
- **Urgent ≠ important.** Most people spend the day on "urgent but not important," and keep pushing "important but not urgent" away.
- What actually shapes your life is Quadrant 2 (important, not urgent): health, relationships, learning, long-term bets.
- The goal: **grow Quadrant 2, shrink Quadrant 1, delete 3 and 4.**

## Notes

**The four quadrants**
| Quadrant | Type | What to do |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Urgent & important (crises, deadlines) | Do it now — but ask why it became urgent |
| 2 | Important, not urgent (planning, learning, exercise) | Schedule it and protect it |
| 3 | Urgent, not important (most meetings, noise) | Delegate, decline, or batch |
| 4 | Neither (doomscrolling, busywork) | Cut it |

**Key insight**
- A lot of Quadrant 1 fires are just Quadrant 2 work you skipped. **Invest in Quadrant 2 early and you fight fewer fires later.**

## Takeaways
- Put three "important, not urgent" tasks straight onto your calendar this week.
- Next time someone hands you a task, ask: which quadrant is this?
