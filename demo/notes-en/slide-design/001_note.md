# 2.1 One idea per slide
> Course: Slide Design, Illustrated (sample)

## Key points
- One slide should carry **one message**. Cram in more and you've said nothing.
- The audience can only "read" or "listen" at once — the slide supports your talk, it isn't the script.
- Write the slide's **one-sentence conclusion first**, then decide what goes on it.

## Notes

**Why one idea per slide**
- Short-term memory holds only 3–4 chunks at a time. Five bullets and they remember none.
- Dense ≠ professional. It makes people anxious and glaze over.

**How to do it**
1. For each slide, write "what should the audience remember" in one line.
2. Delete everything unrelated to that line.
3. Make that line big and put it where the eye lands first.

> If a slide needs to say three things, split it into three slides. Slides are cheap; attention isn't.

## Takeaways
- Open your last deck and find slides with 3+ points — split them.
